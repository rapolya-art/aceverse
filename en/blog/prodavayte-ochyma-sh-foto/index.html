Imagine the situation: you are launching a new product. The supplier sent you photos. You open the file, and there is a boring item on a white (or gray) background, shot with "in-your-face" flash.

50 of your competitors have the exact same photos. They don't evoke emotions, don't show the product in life and, frankly, cheapen your brand.

Previously, to fix this, you needed to: hire a photographer, rent a studio, bring the product, shoot, edit. This is weeks of time and thousands of dollars of budget.

Today Aceverse solves this task in hours using generative artificial intelligence. We take mediocre source material and turn it into candy.

Let's consider a specific transformation case.

Stage 1. Before: Photo "Like Everyone Else's"

We have a source image. This is a standard "packshot" — a photo of a product (for example, sneakers, perfume bottle or smart watch) on an isolated background.

It is informative, but dead. It doesn't sell a lifestyle, it simply captures the presence of the product. If you launch such a photo in ads on Facebook or Instagram, CTR (click-through rate) will be minimal, because the user's eye just slides past.

BEFORE: Regular photo from supplier

Stage 2. After: Studio Quality Without a Studio

We load this photo into our AI tools. Our task is to place this real product in a new, attractive environment without distorting its appearance.

What AI does:

  • Preserves geometry and texture: Most important for goods — the product must look exactly like in life. We generate not a "similar" sneaker, we use exactly yours.
  • Creates context (Lifestyle): We transfer the sneaker to the wet asphalt of a night city, a perfume bottle — to a marble table surrounded by flowers, and furniture — into a stylish interior.
  • Adds professional lighting: AI draws realistic shadows, glare and reflections from the new environment. The product starts looking expensive.
AFTER: AI generation of studio quality

Stage 3. Magic: Transforming Photo into Video

But we don't stop at statics. Social networks (TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts) require dynamics. Static pictures in ads work worse than video.

Using advanced Video AI models (like Runway or Pika), we "animate" our studio photo:

  • Add camera movement (slow zoom in or fly around the product).
  • Create atmospheric effects: light steam from hot coffee, dynamic shadows from leaves, movement of water or light glare in the background.

We received a full-fledged promotional video lasting 5-10 seconds, having only one mediocre photo at the input.
